About AIDS

AIDS (Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome) is a disease caused by the HIV virus. It attacks the immune system, making the body vulnerable to infections and diseases. AIDS is spread through contact with infected bodily fluids such as blood, semen, vaginal fluids, and breast milk.

Prevention

  • Use condoms during sexual intercourse.
  • Avoid sharing needles and syringes.
  • Get tested regularly and know your partner's HIV status.
  • Ensure blood products are tested for HIV.
  • Practice safe sex and educate others about HIV/AIDS.
